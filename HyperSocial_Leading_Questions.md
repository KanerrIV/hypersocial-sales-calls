# HyperSocial Sales Call — Leading Questions Reference
### Consultative Discovery & Direction Guide

> **Core Principle:** Never pitch before you've diagnosed. These questions exist to surface their pain — so when you present the solution, they already know why they need it.
>
> Ask one question at a time, then shut up and listen.

---

## Stage 1 — Rapport & Small Talk *(0–3 min)*

*Goal: Make them like you before you ask them to trust you. Don't skip this.*

| # | Question | Why You're Asking |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Where are you calling in from today? | Opens conversation naturally, gives you something to comment on |
| 2 | How's the weather out there? | Low-stakes, easy to answer — lowers their guard immediately |
| 3 | What are you up to this week outside of work? | Makes you human. Share something back before they answer. |
| 4 | How did you find out about us — was it an ad, LinkedIn, a referral? | Qualifies the lead source and gives you context |

> 💡 Share something about yourself first before asking. The prospect opens up when you go first.

---

## Stage 2 — Discovery Opener *(3–8 min)*

*Goal: Understand why they showed up and what's not working. One opening question, then get out of the way.*

| # | Question | Why You're Asking |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | What made you want to schedule this call? | Most important opener — lets them tell you their problem in their own words |
| 2 | What have you been doing for new business, and why are you looking for something different now? | Reveals current methods AND the gap — the "why now" is critical |
| 3 | Why are you currently looking for something for lead generation? | Shorter version if they seem like a direct communicator |
| 4 | What's your primary source of new clients right now? | Identifies referral dependency — their most common pain point |
| 5 | How predictable is that pipeline month to month? | Surfaces the unpredictability problem — gets them to say it themselves |

> 💡 After they answer, pause. Resist the urge to jump in. Let silence do the work — they'll often keep talking and give you the real answer.

---

## Stage 2B — Deep Discovery Digs

*Goal: Once they've opened up, go deeper. Uncover capacity, ICP, geography, and urgency.*

### Business & ICP

| # | Question | Why You're Asking |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Tell me about your average customer. | Defines ICP — critical for campaign targeting |
| 2 | Is that more B2B or B2C? | Confirms LinkedIn is the right channel for them |
| 3 | What industries or types of companies do you go after? | Campaign targeting — Sales Navigator filters |
| 4 | What's the typical revenue or company size of the businesses you target? | Refines headcount/revenue filters in Sales Nav |
| 5 | Do you have more than one type of ideal client you go after? | Flags multiple ICPs — sets up the simultaneous campaigns conversation |
| 6 | Ideally, would you want leads for both [X] and [Y]? | Confirms multi-campaign need — use when you spot multiple ICPs |

### Geography & Scale

| # | Question | Why You're Asking |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Are you building locally, regionally, or are you open to going national? | Shapes targeting strategy — local first builds stronger second-degree networks |
| 2 | Are there specific cities or markets you'd want to go after first? | Gets them thinking concretely about sequencing campaigns |
| 3 | Are there markets you've already tapped that we'd want to exclude? | Introduces the blacklist feature naturally |

### Growth & Capacity

| # | Question | Why You're Asking |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | How many new clients would actually move the needle for you each month? | Anchors the value of 3–6 appts/week — makes the numbers hit harder |
| 2 | Are you at capacity right now, or do you have room to bring on new clients? | Surfaces the ceiling problem — or confirms they're ready for volume |
| 3 | If your pipeline doubled tomorrow, could your team handle it? | Higher-stakes version of capacity question — good for solo operators |
| 4 | What's keeping you from growing right now — is it a lead problem, a capacity problem, or something else? | Clarifies root cause — ensures LinkedIn actually solves their problem |

---

## Stage 2C — Prior Experience Questions

*Goal: Understand what they've tried. Protects you from blindsides and lets you pre-handle objections.*

| # | Question | Why You're Asking |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Have you done anything with LinkedIn before for lead gen? | Flags if they've tried a tool before — sets up the differentiation conversation |
| 2 | What did you try and what happened? | Lets them vent — then you can acknowledge and reframe |
| 3 | Have you ever used a lead gen agency or outsourced this before? | Anchors the value vs. $4K/month agency fees |
| 4 | What's worked even a little bit in the past that you'd want to keep doing? | Shows respect for their effort — you can layer LinkedIn on top of it |
| 5 | Do you have any events or webinars coming up that you'd want to promote? | Opens the door to the LinkedIn event invitation feature |

> 💡 If they've tried LinkedIn automation before and got burned: *"You're right that LinkedIn has changed a lot. Here's what's different about what we do..."* — then explain the human-written messaging and proprietary software.

---

## Stage 3 — Credibility & Pain Reflection *(8–12 min)*

*Goal: Before you pitch, reflect their situation back. Confirms you listened and earns the right to present.*

| # | Question / Statement | Why You're Using It |
|---|----------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | So if I'm hearing you right — most of your business comes from referrals, it's been great, but it's unpredictable. Is that fair? | Summarizes their pain back — they'll almost always say "exactly" |
| 2 | Sounds like the last hat you want to put on is the prospecting hat — you'd rather be doing the work you're good at. Right? | Reframes busyness as a pain point without them having to say it |
| 3 | You know LinkedIn is the right place to be — you just don't know where to start. Is that about right? | Classic credibility bridge — confirms they're already sold on the channel |
| 4 | We've worked with [X type of client] a lot — does your situation look similar to what I described? | Social proof + ICP fit confirmation in one question |

---

## Stage 4 — Pitch Checkpoint Questions

*Goal: During the pitch, check for understanding and buy-in. Don't lecture — make it a conversation.*

| # | Question | Why You're Asking |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Does that targeting capability make sense for what you're trying to do? | Confirms Sales Navigator's value for their specific ICP |
| 2 | How does your LinkedIn profile look right now — do you feel like it represents you well? | Surfaces the profile optimization need without being insulting |
| 3 | Does 3–6 appointments a week sound like it would move the needle for your business? | Ties the pitch numbers directly back to their stated goal |
| 4 | Is content something you've tried to be consistent with, or has that been a challenge? | Opens the 20-posts-written conversation — makes it a relief, not a feature |
| 5 | Do you have a CRM you're working out of right now? | Surfaces the Zapier/integration conversation before it becomes an objection |
| 6 | Does the video messaging piece make sense for someone in your space? | Especially powerful for coaches and consultants — gets them to visualize it |

---

## Stage 5 — Objection-Handling Questions

*Goal: When an objection comes up, ask before you answer. Understanding the real concern beats having a quick response.*

| # | Question | Why You're Asking |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | What's your biggest hesitation right now? | Gets the real objection out on the table — they may be holding back |
| 2 | Is it more of a timing thing, or is there something about the approach that doesn't feel right? | Separates "not yet" from "not this" — very different conversations |
| 3 | What would need to be true for this to feel like a no-brainer? | Inverts the objection — lets them define their own buying criteria |
| 4 | If cash flow is the only thing in the way, would a payment plan change that? | Opens payment plan without them having to ask for it |
| 5 | Have you made investments like this in your business before? | Addresses the "is this worth $2,500" question through their history, not your defense |
| 6 | What would it cost you to not solve this problem over the next 12 months? | Reframes the investment through the cost of inaction — powerful closer |

---

## Stage 6 — Pricing & Close

*Goal: State price confidently, ground it in value, then ask the direct question. Silence after you ask.*

| # | Question / Statement | Why You're Using It |
|---|----------------------|---------------------|
| 1 | Before I go over pricing — does everything we've talked about sound like it would work for your situation? | Pre-closes on fit before revealing price — surfaces remaining objections early |
| 2 | Is there anyone else who'd be part of this decision, or is this something you can move on today? | Flushes the "I need to talk to my partner" objection before it appears |
| 3 | Want to move forward with us? | The close. Say it, then stop talking. Silence. |
| 4 | Would it help to split that into two or three payments? | *"I don't want cash to be the reason this doesn't happen."* |
| 5 | Do you have your calendar in front of you? Let's get your welcome call locked in right now. | Locks in next step before they hang up — non-negotiable |

---

## Stage 8 — Post-Close & Referral Questions

*Goal: Keep the relationship warm, plant the referral seed, leave them excited.*

| # | Question | Why You're Asking |
|---|----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Is there anyone in your network who you think could benefit from something like this? | Referral seed — plant it while they're still in a yes mindset |
| 2 | Do you know any other [TAB owners / coaches / consultants] who've been talking about their lead gen? | ICP-specific referral prompt — much more likely to produce names |
| 3 | Any questions before the welcome call — anything you want to make sure we cover? | Keeps engagement high between now and the next touchpoint |

---

## Quick Reference — Questions by Situation

**They're totally referral-dependent:**
> "How predictable is that pipeline month to month?" → "What happens when referrals slow down?" → "How many new clients would move the needle if you had a consistent stream?"

**They're a solo operator / too busy to prospect:**
> "What does your week look like — are you more in delivery mode or do you have time for business development?" → "What would it feel like to have leads coming in without you having to chase them?"

**They've tried LinkedIn automation before:**
> "What tool did you use?" → "What happened with results?" → "You're right that LinkedIn has changed a lot — here's exactly what's different about how we approach it..."

**They have multiple ICPs:**
> "Ideally would you want leads for both [X] and [Y]?" → "We can run simultaneous campaigns and split the 200 weekly requests however makes sense — want me to walk you through how that works?"

**They hesitate on price:**
> "Is it more about the investment itself, or are there other factors?" → "Would a payment plan help?" → "What would it cost you to not solve this over the next year?"

**They seem interested but stalling:**
> "What would need to be true for this to feel like a clear yes?" → "Is there something I haven't addressed yet that's giving you pause?"

---

*Questions are buying signals — answer them fully, never deflect. The close is always the same: welcome call → agreement → form → go.*
