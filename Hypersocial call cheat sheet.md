# HyperSocial Sales Call — Cheat Sheet

---

## Before the Call

- Know their name, company, and how they found you (ad, LinkedIn, referral?)
- Note any ICP clues from the booking form
- Have Brittany's calendar open and ready to book before you hang up

---

## Stage 1 — Rapport *(0–3 min)*

Comment on location, weather, something visible. Share something about yourself first.

> *"Where are you calling in from?"* / *"How's the weather over there?"*

**Don't skip this. They need to like you before they'll trust you.**

---

## Stage 2 — Discovery Opener

**Never pitch first. Always open with a question.**

Pick one:
- *"What made you want to schedule this?"*
- *"Why are you currently looking for something for lead generation?"*
- *"What have you been doing for new business, and why now are you looking for something different?"*

**Then shut up and listen.**

### Listen For:
- [ ] Current lead gen methods (referrals? networking? cold email? events?)
- [ ] Why it's not enough (unpredictable? time? not scaling?)
- [ ] Capacity situation (solo? team? too busy to prospect?)
- [ ] Geography (local only? national? both?)
- [ ] Multiple ICPs (TAB + StratPro, Vistage + advisory, clients + referral partners)
- [ ] Prior LinkedIn tool experience?

### Follow-Up Digs:
- *"How many new clients would move the needle for you monthly?"*
- *"Is that B2B or B2C?"*
- *"Tell me about your average customer."*
- *"Are you building locally, nationally, or both?"*
- *"Ideally, would you want leads for both [X] and [Y]?"*

---

## Stage 3 — Credibility Bridge

Before pitching, connect their world to yours.

- Worked with 65+ TAB owners / Vistage chairs / coaches / consultants
- Corporate TAB pays for new owners to work with us based on results
- *"That's exactly the revenue range our ideal client falls into for ourselves."*
- If they've used LinkedIn tools before: *"You're correct that LinkedIn has changed a lot — here's what's different about what we do."*

**Reflect their pain back:**
> *"You come in at about the same spot most people do — referrals are great but unpredictable, you know LinkedIn is right, you just don't know where to start."*

---

## Stage 4 — The Pitch

### The 30-Day Build Includes:
- [ ] LinkedIn profile audit + optimization
- [ ] Sales Navigator search setup (granular targeting — location, industry, headcount, role)
- [ ] Human-written outreach messaging (no AI — get their approval before anything goes live)
- [ ] Email sequences added alongside LinkedIn
- [ ] Video messaging (their profile populates in background — looks personalized, record once)
- [ ] First 20 LinkedIn posts written + AI content voice trained
- [ ] Campaign live within **7 days** of first call

### The Numbers Funnel:

| Step | Number |
|------|--------|
| Connection requests/week | 200 |
| Monthly requests | ~800–1,000 |
| Connection rate (30%) | ~300 new connections |
| Response rate (30%) | ~90–100 replies |
| Avg. appointments/week | 3–6 |

### End-of-Build Options:
- **Run it yourself** → $99/mo software + ~$100/mo Sales Navigator
- **HyperSocial manages it** → $500–$2,000/mo (custom scope)
  - $500 = outbound only
  - ~$1,000 = outbound + inbox
  - $1,997 = full management (outbound, inbox, content, leads)

### Key Lines to Know:

**LinkedIn vs. cold email:**
> *"Cold email is I have to get your attention now or never. On LinkedIn you can showcase your work and results — when you reach out, people go view your profile and see all that social proof."*

**Blank connection requests:**
> *"10–15% higher connection rate with no message — they don't know why you're connecting, they think they met you somewhere, and they accept to find out."*

**Human-written messaging:**
> *"LinkedIn messaging is harder than any other type of copy. We don't use AI for outreach — everything is written by our team, approved by you before it goes live."*

**Content posting:**
> *"Three posts per week is optimal — longest shelf life of any social platform. Posting too much actually hurts the previous post."*

**Sales Navigator:**
> *"Required for all clients — better filters and LinkedIn gives paying users more daily actions."*

**Webinars/events (relationship businesses):**
> *"You can invite 1,000 first-degree connections to a LinkedIn event weekly. One hour in front of people you couldn't get attention from otherwise — our software automates all the invites."*

## Stage 5 — Common Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Multiple ICPs | Run simultaneous campaigns, split 200 requests however makes sense. Blacklist to exclude current clients/members. |
| CRM integration | Zapier connects to any CRM. Only push responders/interested people — not everyone who connects. CSV download also available. |
| Time commitment | ~1 hr/week during build, one-on-one with account manager. After that, step in only when someone responds + upload leads ~once/month. |
| Is messaging AI? | No — all human-written. AI only used to train content voice for posts, never for outreach. |
| New/inexperienced rep | Acknowledge it directly, explain their background, offer to involve Emily (COO) for the welcome call if needed. |
| Payment plan | Yes — split into 2 or 3 payments. *"I don't want cash to be the reason this doesn't happen."* |
| Second campaign / different offer | No additional setup fee once the system is built. Split the 200 weekly requests across campaigns however they want. |
| Timing / not ready yet | *"This is actually the best time — Q1 budgets are opening up, you hit the ground running."* Soft deadline on promo pricing if applicable. |

---

## Stage 6 — Pricing

**State it flat and confident:**
> *"Just a one-time fee of $2,500."*

Or anchor high first:
> *"Our usual price is $4,500. Right now it's a one-time fee of $2,500."*

**Then immediately ground it:**
- Recurring: ~$200/month total (software + Sales Navigator)
- vs. $4,000/month agency fees — and you own the system
- Effective cost per lead: ~$200/month ongoing

**Payment plan — offer it proactively:**
> *"I'm happy to split that into two or three payments. I don't want cash to be an issue — it also makes the working relationship better."*

---

## Stage 7 — The Close

**Direct ask:**
> *"Want to move forward with us?"* → silence.

**If yes — book the call live, right now:**
1. Pull up Brittany's calendar
2. Schedule 30-min welcome call before hanging up
3. Tell them exactly what's coming:
   - Email with agreement + onboarding form + welcome video
   - Complete before the welcome call
   - Welcome call → launch call ~7 days later → campaigns live

**Never leave without a scheduled next step.**

---

## Stage 8 — Post-Close Wrap

- Wednesday office hours — 2 PM ET weekly, open to all clients
- All sessions recorded and sent to them
- Referral incentive: introduce 3 people via email → next month of software comped
- *"If any questions come up before Monday, feel free to email me."*

---

## What the Onboarding Email Contains

- Intro to account manager (Brittany or Emily)
- Agreement link
- Onboarding form
- Welcome video (Emily)
- Calendar invite for 30-min welcome call

## Quick Reminders

- They need to like you before they trust you — don't rush rapport
- Reflect their pain back before you pitch
- Questions are buying signals — answer them fully, never deflect
- The rep uses this system themselves — mention it when natural
- The close is always the same: welcome call → agreement → form → go
