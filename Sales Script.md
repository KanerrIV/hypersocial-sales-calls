# HyperSocial — Insane Demand Gen
## B2B Closing Script
*Based on the CLOSER Framework | For Sales Reps & Founders*

---

## PRE-CALL PREP CHECKLIST

Do this before every call. The best closers show up prepared.

- Log the lead in your CRM. Create a deal. Tag the source (LinkedIn / cold email / referral).
- Pull up their LinkedIn profile — have it open during the call.
- Visit their company website. Know what they do, who they sell to.
- Note their business type: coaching, agency, SaaS, consulting, financial services, etc.
- Identify 1–2 likely pain points: no consistent pipeline, spending too much time on outreach, not hitting monthly revenue targets, no CRM or sales system in place.
- Choose 1–2 HyperSocial client stories that match their profile (Carlos R., Zander F., Mark R., Chad W.).
- Review the Insane Demand Gen pitch deck so the KPIs and process are fresh.

---

## C — CLARIFY: Open the Call & Establish Intent

**Goal:** Break the ice fast, then get them to articulate why they showed up. You need them to say the problem out loud — that's where the sale starts.

| What You Say | Why You Say It |
|---|---|
| **Small Talk (60–90 seconds max)**<br>"Hey [Name], good to connect. How are things going over at [Company]?"<br><br>If you saw something on their LinkedIn: "I saw you recently [attended X / posted about Y / expanded your team] — how's that been going?" | You want them to feel like you actually looked at their profile, not like you run a boiler room. One personalised line separates you from every other vendor they've talked to.<br><br>Keep it brief. B2B buyers are busy and they're here to solve a business problem, not chat. As soon as they seem ready, transition. |
| **Transition to Intent**<br>"Before we dive in — what made you decide to book this call? What's the problem we're trying to solve today?" | Don't assume. Let them tell you the gap. This sets up everything that comes next and forces them to say the pain out loud, which creates psychological ownership of it. |
| **If They're Vague — Pain Scan**<br>"Totally understand. Usually when someone at [their role / company type] books a call with us, it's because one of two things: either their pipeline is inconsistent month to month, or they're spending way too much time manually doing outreach with no system in place. Does either of those sound familiar?" | This is your 'floor' — it surfaces the most common pains for their profile without them having to spell it out. It also signals you've worked with people like them before. |

## L — LAY OUT THE AGENDA: Take Control of the Call

**Goal:** Signal professionalism and get permission to ask questions. This prevents the call from becoming a free product demo with no close.

| What You Say | Why You Say It |
|---|---|
| "Perfect. Here's how I want to make this worth your time today."<br><br>"I'll spend about five minutes understanding your current situation — the way you're generating leads right now, what's working, what isn't."<br><br>"Then I'll walk you through exactly how HyperSocial works, what results our clients have seen, and whether this is actually a good fit for you."<br><br>"Sound good?" | Laying out a structure does two things: it positions you as an expert (not a salesperson), and it gives them confidence the call won't run long.<br><br>Asking 'sound good?' gets a micro-commitment. They've now agreed to let you run the show. |

---

## O — OVERVIEW PAIN: Uncover What's Actually Broken

**Goal:** Use SPICED-style questions to get them talking about the real cost of their current situation. The more specific they get, the easier the close.

> 💡 Don't rush this section. Let them talk. The pain they surface here is what you'll use word-for-word when you pitch the solution.

| What You Say | Why You Say It |
|---|---|
| **SITUATIONAL — Current Reality**<br>"Walk me through how you're currently generating new B2B clients. What's your process right now?" | You need to know their baseline. Are they cold calling? Running ads? Posting on LinkedIn and hoping? This tells you exactly what you're replacing or complementing. |
| **PAIN — Where It's Breaking Down**<br>"And is that process giving you a predictable number of new clients each month, or does it feel more hit-or-miss?"<br><br>"How much time per week would you estimate you or your team spends on outreach and business development?" | These reveal whether the problem is volume, predictability, or time drain. All three are pain points HyperSocial directly addresses — but you want their number, not your guess. |
| **IMPACT — What It's Actually Costing Them**<br>"What does that inconsistency cost you? If you had two or three more qualified conversations on your calendar every week, what would that mean for your revenue this quarter?" | This is where the emotional and financial weight lands. Let them calculate the opportunity cost themselves. A prospect who says 'that would mean $30K/month more' has just told you what the solution is worth. |
| **CRITICAL EVENT — Timeline**<br>"Is there a particular timeline here — a revenue goal you're trying to hit, a quarter you need to turn around, or a point where this becomes critical to figure out?" | Urgency is rarely spontaneous. You need to find or create a natural deadline. A Q3 goal, a new hire they need to fund, a competitor gaining ground — these all create a reason to move now, not later. |
| **DECISION — How They'll Choose**<br>"When you're evaluating whether a solution like this is right for you, what matters most? Is it speed to results, the ROI math, having a done-for-you system, or something else?" | Their answer here tells you how to frame your pitch and which proof points to lead with. Don't guess their decision criteria — ask. |

## S — SELL: Present the Solution Against Their Pain

**Goal:** Mirror their exact pain back to them, then show them HyperSocial is the bridge between where they are and where they want to go. No generic pitch — tie everything to what they just told you.

> 💡 Use this transition line to open the pitch.

| What You Say | Why You Say It |
|---|---|
| **Bridge to the Pitch**<br>"This actually reminds me of [client profile]."<br><br>"We work with a lot of [coaches / agency owners / consultants / B2B service companies] who come to us in exactly that situation — strong offer, good reputation, but no reliable system for getting in front of the right decision-makers on a consistent basis."<br><br>"What we do at HyperSocial is build that system for them." | The 'reminds me of' opener signals experience and pattern recognition. They hear 'you've helped someone like me before,' which builds credibility before you've said a single feature.<br><br>Note: You don't need to name the exact client — just describe someone who had their situation. |
| **The Three-Phase System**<br><br>**Phase 1 — Find Decision Makers:**<br>"We start by optimising your LinkedIn profile so it positions you as an authority, then we build a targeted audience of your ideal prospects — the actual decision-makers at the companies you want to work with. No spray-and-pray. Specific titles, industries, company sizes."<br><br>**Phase 2 — Your Lead Gen System:**<br>"Then we build and run a fully automated outreach system — LinkedIn connection sequences, follow-up messaging, and a cold email campaign running in parallel. All of it flows into a CRM we set up for you. The goal is to have qualified conversations arriving in your inbox without you manually doing any of the outreach."<br><br>**Phase 3 — Sales Scripts & Templates:**<br>"And we equip you with the sales scripts, canned responses, and closing framework to convert those conversations into clients. Our clients typically see 1–5 new proposals per month by the end of the first 30 days." | Walk through the three phases as a story: Find → Connect → Close.<br><br>**KPIs to reference if they ask:**<br>• 800 connection requests/month<br>• ~10% connection rate<br>• ~24% reply rate<br>• $0.70–$2.30 cost per lead<br>• $15.30 cost per meaningful conversation<br><br>These numbers do two things: they make the system feel real and measurable, and they make the ROI math easy. |
| **What's Included — The Stack**<br>"Here's exactly what you get: LinkedIn profile audit and optimisation, target audience build, fully written outreach and cold email copy, CRM setup, campaign build and monitoring, 1-on-1 Q&A sessions, sales training, and access to our resource vault with all the templates and swipe files."<br><br>"Total value of everything in that package is $26,500. Our price is $4,500 for the 30-day build and launch." | Itemising the value stack justifies the price before you say it. When they hear $4,500 after hearing $26,500 across 10+ deliverables, the investment feels rational.<br><br>You don't have to memorise every line item — keep the list handy and reference it naturally. |
| **Social Proof — Drop a Client Story**<br><br>**Example A (Fractional / Agency):**<br>"One of our clients, Carlos — copywriter and fractional CMO — had never gotten LinkedIn to work. Within a few weeks of launching with us, he'd closed a fractional CMO retainer at $7,500 a month."<br><br>**Example B (Coaching / Consulting):**<br>"Zander runs a high-impact coaching business. He knew he needed to maximise LinkedIn but hadn't cracked it. He started getting 12+ qualified meetings every month and is now consistently closing new business — $18,000 a month in new revenue."<br><br>**Example C (Agency / Introverted Founder):**<br>"Mark runs a YouTube ads agency. Great at the work, but identified as an introvert and hated traditional sales. He had no real lead gen process. Within 30 days he had $47,900 in new proposals in his pipeline." | Match the story to your prospect's profile. If they run a service business, use Carlos. If they're a coach or speaker, use Zander. If they're an agency owner, use Mark.<br><br>The stories work because they describe the situation *before* HyperSocial, not just the result after. Prospects self-identify. |

## E — EMPATHISE: Handle Objections with Confidence

Pause before the close and invite questions. In B2B, unresolved objections kill deals that should have closed — they just resurface later. Surface them now.

**Objection Opener:**

> *"Before I walk through next steps — based on what you've heard, do you have any questions? Any concerns about whether this makes sense for your business?"*

| Objection | Your Response |
|---|---|
| **"It's not the right time."** | "I hear that — timing is always a factor. Can I ask: what would need to be different for the timing to be right?"<br><br>"The reason I ask is that most of our clients say the same thing before starting, but the system takes 14–30 days to go live. Whatever's on your plate right now will still be on your plate in 30 days — except you could have a full pipeline running in parallel." |
| **"We already do LinkedIn / outreach in-house."** | "That's great — and that's actually our favourite situation to walk into. Most companies doing it in-house are generating some activity, but not with a proven sequence, not with a CRM tracking everything, and not at the volume this system runs."<br><br>"We're not replacing your team — we're giving them a system that multiplies what they're already doing." |
| **"$4,500 is more than we budgeted."** | "Understood. Let me put that in context for a second."<br><br>"You mentioned [X revenue goal / Y new clients per month]. If this system generates even one new client in the first 30 days — and at your [price point], that covers the investment in full. The question isn't whether $4,500 is a lot of money. The question is whether one closed deal makes this worth it."<br><br>"What's your average client value?" |
| **"I need to think about it."** | "Of course. What specifically do you want to think through? I'd rather address that now so you're not sitting on questions later."<br><br>"Is it the ROI, the process, the timing, or something else?" |
| **"I need to talk to my partner / team."** | "Makes sense — this is a business decision. When can you get them on a call with us? I'd love to walk them through the same thing in 20 minutes so they have the full picture."<br><br>"Would [specific day] work to get everyone on?" |
| **"How do I know this will work for my niche?"** | "Fair question. We've run this for coaches, consultants, agencies, SaaS companies, financial advisors, commercial contractors, HR firms, and more. The LinkedIn audience targeting is niche-specific — we build your list around the exact decision-makers in your industry."<br><br>"What does your ideal client look like? Let me tell you if we've done it before." |

## R — REINFORCE: Map Out Next Steps & Close

**Goal:** Get a clear yes, a clear no, or a defined follow-up — never leave the call with vague 'we'll be in touch.' Either they're in or they need one more thing. Find out which.

> 💡 The easiest close in B2B: give them two paths. Not 'are you ready to buy?' — that's too abrupt. Instead, set up a binary.

| What You Say | Why You Say It |
|---|---|
| **The Close**<br>"Here's where I want to land before we wrap up."<br><br>"Based on everything we've covered today, it sounds like [pain point 1] and [pain point 2] are real blockers for you right now, and what we do directly solves both of those."<br><br>"So here's where we are: either this makes sense and we move forward — in which case I walk you through onboarding today and you're live within 14 days."<br><br>"Or something's still off, and I want to know what that is so we can address it."<br><br>"Which one are we closer to?" | This close is low-pressure because it's just asking for honest feedback. If they say 'something's off,' you have an objection to handle. If they say 'we're close to moving forward,' you have a soft yes to confirm.<br><br>Mirror their own words: use the exact pain points they mentioned, not generic ones. |
| **If YES — Confirm and Move Fast**<br>"Great. Here's what happens next: I'll send you over the agreement and the onboarding form today. Once that's done, our team creates your Slack channel, schedules your 60-minute onboarding call, and we're live within two weeks."<br><br>"Any questions about the onboarding before I send that over?" | Momentum matters. The more concrete and immediate the next step feels, the less room there is for second-guessing.<br><br>HyperSocial's onboarding timeline: Day 1 onboarding call → Day 5–7 review → Day 12–14 go live. |
| **If NOT YET — Define the Follow-Up**<br>"I understand. What's the one thing that would need to be true for this to be a yes?"<br><br>"I can get you the pitch deck and a written summary of everything we covered — does that help?"<br><br>"If I send that over today, when can I expect to hear back? And would it be OK for me to follow up with you directly if I don't?" | Never leave a 'maybe' without a hard date. 'I'll think about it' becomes a dead lead without a specific follow-up time agreed on the call.<br><br>Offering a follow-up nudge and getting permission for it signals confidence without being pushy. |

## POST-CALL ACTION LIST

Do this within one hour of ending the call.

- Log call notes in CRM — record exact pain points in their words.
- Update deal stage and set a follow-up task with a specific date.
- Send the pitch deck and a short personalised video summarising the call and next steps.
- Connect on LinkedIn — opens a second channel for follow-up.
- If they said yes: send the agreement and onboarding form same day.
- If they said not yet: send a one-line email confirming the follow-up date you agreed on.
